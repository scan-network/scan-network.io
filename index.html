About SCAN

SCAN - Smart Cities Air Quality Network is an open-source community of individuals from academia, public services, and practice involved in researching and disseminating information on air quality challenges worldwide. SCAN helps connect researchers from research, policy, industry and the third sector with expertise on outdoor and indoor air quality challenges.

History of SCAN Network

SCAN was born from the desire of the project partners working on Air quality challenges in Smart Cities and Open Data REuse (SCORE https://northsearegion.eu/score ) project to continue collaboration and knowledge sharing on Air quality-related challenges. .

What does SCAN Do?

  • Maintains the directory of Smart cities projects focusing on outdoor and indoor air quality challenges.
  • Maintains the directory of experts and/or interested individuals on outdoor and indoor air quality challenges.
  • Provides a platform for knowledge exchange from these projects, practitioners, and researchers through
  • EVENTS - Various SCAN events, including seminars, meetings, and workshops.
  • OPEN-SOURCE REPOSITORY - of data, articles (significant news, research articles, breakthroughs, experiments and experience sharing articles), SCAN event reports disseminated through quarterly newsletters.
